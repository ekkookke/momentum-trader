# Keep signal expressions market-data-only

Accepted. Entry Signal and Exit Signal should share the same Signal Rule Expression abstraction: both are market-data expressions over Tradable Daily Bars and derived market indicators, and neither should read current holdings or portfolio state. Strategy Execution Filters own state-aware decisions such as current position, exposure limits, staged-entry state, cooldown rules, and whether a market signal should become an Action Candidate or simulated trade.

**Considered Options**

- Let Signal Conditions directly depend on holdings, Pyramiding state, portfolio exposure, and other strategy execution state.
- Keep Signal Rule Expressions market-data-only and apply state-aware filters in the Strategy Execution layer.

**Consequences**

This keeps the signal layer reviewable and reusable for scans, reports, and backtests, while leaving room for state-aware execution discipline outside the signal expression. Formal Signal Conditions may read standard market fields and signal-layer-derived indicators, but they must not accept arbitrary precomputed factor columns unless source, observation time, and lag semantics are explicit enough to rule out look-ahead. Formal signal semantics are daily and after-close in the first system version; T-day OHLCV may be used only after the T-day bar is complete and does not imply T-day-open execution is available. Weekly, monthly, or intraday signals require a separate future design rather than implicit reuse of daily Signal Observation Time. Cross-series market inputs, such as Benchmark-relative or ETF-relative comparisons, must be aligned by Signal Observation Time and should not be forward-filled for formal signal evaluation; a missing comparison-series value makes the affected condition Unknown. Universe-level ranking rules such as Top N momentum selection may use signal-layer metrics, but the ranking/filtering itself belongs to Strategy Execution Filters or candidate selection rather than Signal Rule Expressions. Same-day tie-breaking and priority among multiple true Entry Signals also belong to Strategy Execution Filters or candidate selection. Next-bar execution barriers such as suspension, missing open price, limit-up open, cash limits, or position-slot limits affect Execution Eligibility and skipped-order explanations; they do not rewrite the underlying Signal Result. Exit Signals may be calculated as market observations for ETFs that are not currently held, but only Strategy Execution Filters can turn them into sell-side Action Candidates for existing positions. Drawdown or trailing stops depend on holding-path state such as entry date, Pyramiding state, and peak price, so they belong to Strategy Execution Filters or risk-exit rules rather than Signal Conditions. The current implementation's exit `apply=before_pyramid_complete` and `apply=after_pyramid_complete` configuration, plus its `trailing_stop` entry under exit conditions, are known layering exceptions; they should be treated as cleanup targets, not precedents for adding more state into Signal Conditions.

Formal entry breakout thresholds must be built from completed bars before the evaluated bar. Current-bar-inclusive breakout calculations may be useful diagnostics, but they should not be treated as formal entry breakout signals because the evaluated bar would help create its own threshold.

First-version formal Entry Signals and Exit Signals are close-confirmed. High or low fields may define thresholds or diagnostics, but using an intraday high/low touch as an entry or exit trigger would require a separate future observation and execution design. Path-dependent risk exits remain outside Signal Conditions.
